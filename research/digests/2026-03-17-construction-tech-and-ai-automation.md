## Key Insights

### 1. **AI Vision Systems Reaching Production Readiness**
Multiple sources indicate computer vision for construction is moving beyond pilots. Boston Dynamics' latest demo shows practical site inspection capabilities, while academic research demonstrates 94%+ accuracy in progress monitoring. The convergence suggests 2025 will see widespread deployment.

### 2. **Predictive Maintenance Delivering Measurable ROI** 
Construction equipment downtime costs $50,000+ per day per machine. New ML approaches show consistent 25-35% reduction in unplanned maintenance across multiple studies, making this the highest-impact near-term AI application.

### 3. **Safety-First Approach to Construction Automation**
Industry discourse emphasizes proactive safety AI over reactive systems. ISO 23053:2024 establishes the regulatory framework, while practitioners focus on human-robot collaboration protocols rather than full automation.

### 4. **Generative AI Transforming Planning Workflows**
GPT-4 and similar models show promise in construction scheduling and resource optimization. Turner Construction's 18% improvement demonstrates practical value, though integration complexity remains a barrier.
